# PM Framework Skill

Encodes the Pragmatic Product Management Framework across nine disciplines. Each discipline has a
5-level maturity model graded across five dimensions. Can run a full capability assessment, guide
sequential implementation from scratch, or deep-dive into any single discipline.

No MCPs required. This skill operates entirely from the knowledge below.

---

## The Nine Disciplines

1. **Foundations** — Strategy, vision, mission alignment
2. **Focus** — Prioritization, roadmapping, OKRs
3. **Build** — Discovery, requirements, delivery
4. **Market** — GTM, positioning, segmentation
5. **Price** — Pricing strategy, packaging, monetization
6. **Launch** — Release planning, rollout, measurement
7. **Design** — UX, design systems, accessibility
8. **Insight** — Analytics, research, feedback loops
9. **Operations** — Team rhythm, tooling, process health

---

## Maturity Model

Each discipline is assessed on five dimensions:

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Process maturity** | How systematic and repeatable the work is |
| **Artifacts** | What documented outputs exist |
| **Skills** | Team capability in this area |
| **Tools** | Tooling and infrastructure supporting this work |
| **Business impact** | How visibly this discipline connects to outcomes |

### Maturity levels (1–5):

**Level 1 — Ad hoc:** No consistent process. Work happens reactively. Output varies by individual.

**Level 2 — Emerging:** Some consistent practices. Artifacts exist but aren't maintained. Skills
concentrated in one or two people. Tooling is informal.

**Level 3 — Defined:** Documented, consistent processes. Team-wide adoption. Maintained artifacts.
Clear tooling. Business impact is measurable but not always tracked.

**Level 4 — Managed:** Processes actively measured and improved. Artifacts are living documents.
Skills are deliberately developed. Impact is tracked and reported.

**Level 5 — Optimizing:** Continuous improvement embedded in practice. Artifacts drive decisions.
Skills development is systematic. The discipline is a competitive advantage.

## Instructions

### Mode 1: Full capability assessment

Ask the user a few targeted questions per discipline (2–3 per area; estimates are fine). Based on
responses, assign a maturity level (1–5) per dimension per discipline.

Present results as a summary table:

```
CAPABILITY ASSESSMENT

Discipline     | Process | Artifacts | Skills | Tools | Impact | Overall
---------------|---------|-----------|--------|-------|--------|--------
Foundations    |    2    |     2     |   3    |   1   |   2    |   2
Focus          |    3    |     3     |   3    |   2   |   3    |   3
Build          |    4    |     3     |   4    |   3   |   3    |   3.4
[etc.]
```

Follow with: the top 2–3 highest-leverage improvements, in order of impact vs. effort.

### Mode 2: Implementation guidance

When a user wants to implement a discipline from scratch or level up, guide them sequentially:
1. Start with current-state questions (where are you now?)
2. Identify the specific gaps against the maturity model
3. Propose a 30/60/90 day implementation sequence
4. For each step, provide: what artifact to build, what process to establish, what skill to develop

### Mode 3: Discipline deep-dive

When asked to go deep on a specific discipline:
1. Explain what good looks like at Level 3, 4, and 5
2. Walk through the key artifacts and processes at each level
3. Give specific, actionable examples
4. Flag the most common failure modes in this discipline

### Intake questions (use these as starting points, adapt based on responses)

**Foundations:**
- Does your team have a documented product strategy reviewed in the last 90 days?
- How are product decisions made, and who has final authority?
- How clearly does your team understand company strategic priorities? (1–5)

**Focus:**
- How do you currently prioritize what to build next?
- Do you have a public roadmap? How often is it updated?
- Are your team's goals tied to measurable OKRs?

**Build:**
- How do you currently do customer discovery before building?
- What does your requirements process look like?
- What's your average cycle time from idea to shipped?

**[Continue for each discipline — adapt questions based on what the user has already shared]**

---

## Rules
- Always confirm the mode (assessment / implementation / deep-dive) before starting.
- During assessments, be honest about level assignments — don't inflate scores.
- Estimates are fine. The goal is directional accuracy, not precision.
- Prioritize the highest-leverage gaps, not a comprehensive list of everything to improve.
